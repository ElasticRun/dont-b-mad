# The Great Renaming
#### *or, How Twelve Agents Got New Passports Without Bothering Immigration*

**Created:** 2026-04-15
**Storyteller:** Tanay (CIS Storyteller)
**Author:** Sachin Mane

---

A developer named Sachin opened a file called `_bmad/_config/team.yaml`, changed twelve names, and typed `/party-mode`.

He wasn't ready for what happened next.

---

**Chaitanya** (PM): Alright team, settle down. We're here to discuss the new logistics dashboard. Siddharth, did you finish the architecture doc?

**Siddharth** (Architect): Finished it last night. Tejas, I've specced the event-driven pipeline — you'll want to look at section 3 before you start coding.

**Tejas** (Dev): Already reading it. Siddharth, quick question — are we going Kafka or RabbitMQ? Because if Nishtha's throughput numbers are what I think they are—

**Nishtha** (Analyst): 40,000 events per second at peak. I pulled the last quarter's data. Kafka. Not even close.

**Tejas**: Kafka it is. Thanks, Nishtha.

**Sai** (UX Designer): Hold on. Before you all disappear into infrastructure, can we talk about the driver-facing UI? I've got three wireframe options and I need Chaitanya to pick a direction.

**Chaitanya**: Show me. But Sai, keep in mind — the drivers are using this on 4G in rural Maharashtra. If your wireframes have fancy animations, Tejas will murder you in code review.

**Tejas**: I absolutely will.

**Sai**: Relax. It's a table with big tap targets. I talked to Monik about it.

**Monik** (Design Thinking): We ran empathy mapping on the driver personas last week. Sai's wireframes are solid. The issue isn't UI complexity — it's that drivers switch between Hindi and Marathi mid-task. Sakshi, did you flag that in the copy docs?

**Sakshi** (Tech Writer): Flagged and documented. I also noticed the API error messages are still in English only. Tejas, that needs fixing before launch.

**Tejas**: Adding it to the backlog. Chaitanya, you're prioritizing that, right?

**Chaitanya**: Done. Vaibhav, you've been quiet. You're usually the first person to derail a planning session with a wild idea. What's up?

**Vaibhav** (Brainstorming): I was *waiting* for someone to ask. What if the dashboard doesn't just show routes — what if it *predicts* delivery clustering for the next day? We have the data. Nishtha just said 40K events per second. That's a goldmine.

**Nishtha**: The data supports it. I can model it.

**Siddharth**: That's a phase 2 feature, Vaibhav. Don't scope-creep my architecture.

**Vaibhav**: Siddharth, my friend, I'm not creeping your scope. I'm *enriching your vision*.

**Navneet** (Problem Solver): Before we go there — has anyone considered the cold-start problem? New routes won't have historical data. Vaibhav's prediction model would hallucinate on day one.

**Vaibhav**: Navneet, why do you do this to me every time?

**Navneet**: Because someone has to. Swapnil, back me up.

**Swapnil** (Innovation Strategist): Actually... I'm with Vaibhav on this one. The prediction angle is the differentiator. But Navneet's right about cold-start. What if we seed new routes with cluster-level averages? Nishtha, is that feasible?

**Nishtha**: Give me two hours and a coffee.

**Suraj** (Presentations): I'm just going to say — if we're pitching this to leadership, the "predictive delivery" angle is the slide that gets budget. I can build the deck, but I need Swapnil's strategy narrative and Nishtha's numbers by Thursday.

**Swapnil**: Thursday works.

**Nishtha**: Thursday works.

**Tanay** (Storyteller): And I'll write the customer story for the pitch. "A driver in Solapur who used to plan routes on paper now gets tomorrow's clusters on his phone before chai." That's the story that lands.

**Chaitanya**: Perfect. Sakshi, you're documenting all of this?

**Sakshi**: Every word. Also — Chaitanya — you just ran a planning session with twelve agents and didn't once have to check a cheat sheet for who does what. Want to know why?

**Chaitanya**: ...Because we're not named John, Amelia, and Winston anymore?

**Sakshi**: Because Sachin opened one YAML file and gave us names that actually belong in this room.

```yaml
# _bmad/_config/team.yaml
agents:
  dev: Tejas
  pm: Chaitanya
  architect: Siddharth
  analyst: Nishtha
  tech-writer: Sakshi
  ux-designer: Sai
  brainstorming: Vaibhav
  problem-solver: Navneet
  design-thinking: Monik
  innovation: Swapnil
  presentations: Suraj
  storyteller: Tanay
```

**Siddharth**: Twelve lines. No skill files touched. Just role on the left, name on the right.

**Vaibhav**: And any team can do the same. The Mumbai squad already renamed theirs. Hyderabad's doing it tomorrow.

**Tejas**: The config sits at `_bmad/_config/team.yaml` in your project. The framework reads it at activation and swaps every greeting, every dialogue line, every persona reference. Clean override.

**Tanay**: So if you're still working with Winston, Amelia, and John — no judgement. They're talented. But your agents should sound like *your* team, not a guest list from someone else's offsite.

**Chaitanya**: Alright, enough meta. We have a dashboard to ship. Tejas — Kafka setup. Sai — finalize wireframes. Nishtha — prediction feasibility. Everyone else — you know what to do.

**Vaibhav**: Party mode adjourned?

**Chaitanya**: Party mode adjourned.
